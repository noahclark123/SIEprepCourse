Memory Aids and Acronyms

Effective techniques to help you remember key concepts for the SIE exam

Regulatory Entities and Agencies

Federal Regulators: "SEC FINRA CFTC"

Think: "Securities Enforcement Cops For Controlling The Capital markets"

This helps you remember the main federal regulators in the securities industry:

  • SEC - Securities and Exchange Commission
  • FINRA - Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
  • CFTC - Commodity Futures Trading Commission

SEC's Three-Part Mission: "PIM"

Think: "Protecting Investors Matters"

This helps you remember the SEC's three-part mission:

  • Protect investors
  • Integrity of markets (maintain fair, orderly, and efficient markets)
  • Make capital formation easier

SROs: "FINRA NYSE MSRB OCC"

Think: "Financial Navigators Make Obvious Choices"

This helps you remember the main Self-Regulatory Organizations:

  • FINRA - Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
  • NYSE - New York Stock Exchange
  • MSRB - Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board
  • OCC - Options Clearing Corporation

Securities Acts and Laws

Securities Acts: "1933, 1934, 1940, 1940"

Think: "During the Great Depression, FDR Fixed Investments And Advisers"

This helps you remember the four major securities acts and their focus:

  • 1933 - Securities Act (regulates new securities issuance and requires registration)
  • 1934 - Securities Exchange Act (regulates secondary market trading and created the SEC)
  • 1940 - Investment Company Act (regulates mutual funds and other investment companies)
  • 1940 - Investment Advisers Act (regulates investment advisers)

Quick Tip:

Remember: "33 is for NEW, 34 is for WHO" - The 1933 Act focuses on new securities issuance, while the 1934 Act focuses on who can trade them (broker-dealers).

Modern Securities Laws: "SOX, JOBS, Dodd-Frank"

Think: "Since Obvious eXcesses, JOBS Depend on Financial reform"

This helps you remember the more recent securities laws:

  • Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) of 2002 - Enhanced corporate governance after Enron
  • Jumpstart Our Business Startups (JOBS) Act of 2012 - Eased regulations for small businesses
  • Dodd-Frank Act of 2010 - Comprehensive financial reform after 2008 crisis

Market Structure

Types of Markets: "PSTF"

Think: "Please Send The Forms"

This helps you remember the four types of markets:

  • Primary Market - Where new securities are issued
  • Secondary Market - Where existing securities are traded
  • Third Market - OTC trading of exchange-listed securities
  • Fourth Market - Direct trading between institutions

Economic Factors: "PRIME"

Think: "PRIME factors affect market conditions"

This helps you remember the key economic factors affecting securities markets:

  • Productivity - Output per unit of input
  • Rates (interest) - Set by the Federal Reserve
  • Inflation - Rising prices of goods and services
  • Monetary Policy - Actions by the Federal Reserve
  • Employment - Jobs and unemployment rate

Products and Their Risks

Equity Securities: "CROPS"

Think: "Companies Raise Ownership through Preferred Stock"

This helps you remember the main types of equity securities:

  • Common Stock - Basic ownership shares
  • Rights - Short-term privilege to buy new shares
  • Options - Right to buy or sell at a specific price
  • Preferred Stock - Hybrid security with debt-like features
  • Special securities (ADRs, warrants) - Other equity-related instruments

Debt Instruments: "TCMA"

Think: "Treasury Corporate Municipal Agency"

This helps you remember the main types of debt securities:

  • Treasury Securities - Issued by U.S. Department of Treasury
  • Corporate Bonds - Issued by corporations
  • Municipal Bonds - Issued by state and local governments
  • Agency Securities - Issued by government-sponsored enterprises

Investment Risks: "CLIMB MPF"

Think: "Careful Investors CLIMB Mountains Properly For success"

This helps you remember the main types of investment risks:

  • Credit/Default Risk - Risk that issuer won't pay
  • Liquidity Risk - Risk of not being able to sell quickly
  • Interest Rate Risk - Risk from changing interest rates
  • Market Risk - Risk from overall market movements
  • Business Risk - Risk from company operations
  • Market Liquidity Risk - Risk of market-wide liquidity issues
  • Political Risk - Risk from government actions
  • Foreign Exchange Risk - Risk from currency fluctuations

Trading and Customer Accounts

Order Types: "MLMS"

Think: "Market Limit Market Stop"

This helps you remember the four basic order types:

  • Market Order - Execute at current market price
  • Limit Order - Execute only at specified price or better
  • Market-on-Close Order - Execute at closing price
  • Stop Order - Becomes market order when price is reached

Account Types: "JCIT"

Think: "Just Check Investment Types"

This helps you remember the main types of customer accounts:

  • Joint Account - Owned by two or more people
  • Cash Account - Must pay in full for securities
  • IRA - Individual Retirement Account
  • Trust Account - Assets held by trustee for beneficiaries

Prohibited Activities: "FIRM"

Think: "Fraudulent Insider trading Ruins Markets"

This helps you remember key prohibited activities:

  • Fraud and misrepresentation - Deceptive practices
  • Insider trading - Trading on material non-public information
  • Registration violations - Acting without proper registration
  • Market manipulation - Artificially affecting security prices

Regulatory Framework

FINRA Rules: "Know Your 2000-3000s"

Think: "2000s for Duties, 3000s for Supervision"

This helps you remember key FINRA rule series:

  • 2000 Series - Duties and Conflicts
  • 2100 Series - Transactions with Customers
  • 2200 Series - Communications and Disclosures
  • 2300 Series - Special Products
  • 3000 Series - Supervision and Responsibilities

Registration Requirements: "FIRE"

Think: "Financial Industry Registration Essentials"

This helps you remember key registration requirements:

  • Form U4 - Uniform Application for Securities Industry Registration
  • Ineligibility - Statutory disqualification conditions
  • Registration categories - Various representative and principal roles
  • Examinations - Required qualification exams (SIE plus specialized exams)
